    Who We Are

    Everything Audio Visual is the preferred Bay Area event production company since 1999. We are audiovisual and multimedia professionals who enjoy creating cool environments, from romantic to hip, using imagination and the latest technology. EAV is engrained in the Bay Area's events scene working closely with the most sophisticated and renowned venues in town. We are honored to be Preferred Vendors at the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art, Asian Art Museum, DeYoung Museum, Legion of Honor, Exploratorium, and San Francisco City Hall, just to name a few.

    Our Mission

    At Everything Audio Visual, we combine education, experience, and excellent service with creativity, care, and competence to create a custom event for you using the latest audio, video, and lighting technologies. We are not only well versed in tech, but also experienced in event production. We get it done so you don’t have to worry about it: It's like an insurance policy for your event!

    Our Vision

    Event work is quite challenging. In the course of a single day, we build your dreams from scratch, rehearse, perform, and truck it all away again. Challenges many might feel daunted by are where we thrive. When a bride and groom or client first lay eyes on the setting for their big day, we are reminded why we do this work. The rewarding feeling of knowing so many will remember the event for a lifetime to come is the reason.

    Great for Any Event.

    With over 20 years experience designing and managing multimedia events, Everything Audio Visual (led by founder Chris Williams) strives to bring you the best events possible. Chris always goes the extra mile for his clients and our staff follows his customer service lead in every way. Everything Audio Visual is known for doing, well, Everything, and doing it right. In an ever-changing environment, we constantly work toward growing our knowledge base and equipment to stay up to date with the latest in technology and trends. For example, we now proudly offer cutting edge Projection Mapping.
